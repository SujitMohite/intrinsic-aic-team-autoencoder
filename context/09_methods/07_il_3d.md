# 3D / Point-Cloud Imitation Learning — DP3, 3D Diffuser Actor, RVT-2, RISE

## TL;DR

Drop 2D images, work in **3D**: scene tokens or point clouds. **DP3** (Ze 2024) uses a tiny sparse-point-cloud encoder + diffusion-policy head, hits 85% on 4 real tasks with **40 demos**. **3D Diffuser Actor** (Ke 2024) builds a 3D feature field for RLBench-SOTA performance. **RVT-2** (Goyal 2024) renders virtual views for coarse-to-fine keypose prediction with 10 demos. **RISE** (Yang 2024) is the clean point-cloud + sparse-3D-encoder + DP head baseline. **All require depth or point cloud, which we do not have natively** on the AIC Gazebo wrist-RGB setup — this is the binding constraint that decides whether this whole family is in scope.

## Why this could work for AIC

- **3D structure is the right level for insertion.** Cable-to-port alignment is fundamentally a 3D geometric problem. 2D image policies have to *learn* 3D structure; 3D policies start there.
- **Sample efficiency is dramatic.** RVT-2 reports plug-insertion-class tasks with **10 demos**. DP3 with 40.
- **Better generalization to camera pose / lighting.** Point cloud features are invariant to those nuisance axes.

## Why this could fail for AIC (skeptical)

- **No depth from Gazebo wrist cams natively.** Cable insertion is observed by 3 RGB cameras at 1152×1024. **We have to manufacture depth somehow:**
  - **Stereo from two cameras** (left + right). Requires camera extrinsic calibration + a depth estimator (off-the-shelf RAFT, MiDaS, or sim-rendered ground-truth depth).
  - **Sim-rendered depth** if we instrument the Gazebo wrist cameras to emit a depth channel. **Cheap if we control the Gazebo launch; available via `gz topic` if the cameras are configured to publish depth.** Real-camera-Phase-2 won't have this; sim-only.
  - **Multi-view triangulation** from our 3 wrist cams. Requires careful calibration.
- **Engineering cost.** Adding depth processing to our LeRobot pipeline is ~1 person-week.
- **3D Diffuser Actor wants 24 GB.** Borderline on 16 GB. Mini-Diffuser (2025 variant) gets RLBench-18 at 95% SOTA on a single 4090 in 13 hours — still our hardware tier; we'd port that.
- **5 mm tolerance unproven in 3D-IL literature** specifically for cable insertion. RVT-2 mentions plugs but not sub-cm.

## Generalization analysis

| Axis | Generalizes? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NIC index 0–4 | strong | 3D handles spatial variation well. |
| Board pose & yaw | very strong | Better than 2D — invariant to camera placement. |
| Plug type (SFP/SC) | strong | Geometry differs, 3D captures it. |
| Grasp-pose noise | moderate-strong | Depth lets the policy see relative plug pose. |
| Lighting / texture | very strong | Geometry is lighting-invariant. |
| Sim-to-real | strong | The headline pitch for 3D-IL. |

## Data needs

- **Type**: (point cloud, action) demos, OR (depth, action).
- **Amount**: 40-100 demos (much smaller than 2D-IL).
- **Collection strategy**: requires the keystone pipeline ([`../10_data/02_offline_scripted_groundtruth.md`](../10_data/02_offline_scripted_groundtruth.md)) **plus depth extraction**:
  - Modify Gazebo cameras to emit depth channel (config in `aic.sdf`).
  - OR run a depth estimator post-hoc on RGB pairs.
  - Store depth as a 4th camera channel in the parquet.
- **Overlap**: same demos as 2D-IL — just a richer observation column. The pipeline is shared.

## Compute & time

- DP3: ~6-8 hours on 16 GB.
- RVT-2 / 3D Diffuser Actor: tight; use Mini-Diffuser variant for our hardware tier.
- Inference: DP3 ~57 ms (compatible with RTC); RVT-2 ~80 ms; 3D Diffuser Actor ~100+ ms.
